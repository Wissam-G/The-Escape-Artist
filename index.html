A Brooklyn girl ran away to Middle East and...

Nakeema Stefflbauer



Nakeema Stefflbauer was born and raised in the Bedford Stuyvesant/Crown Heights section of Brooklyn well before the end of the crack wars and myth of the "welfare queen." Fleeing the grim reality of her surroundings in the local library, she began a journey which led to high school in Manhattan and then the Ivy League, ending at at Harvard University.

Defying well-meaning counselors and integration programs that advised her to limit her ambitions, she ventured to five different Arab countries - in search of a place where she could shine as a black woman. Twenty years later, after a remarkable career in the tech industry, her move to Berlin brought her to the epicenter of the current European refugee crisis. There, she founded an NGO to empower women immigrants and residents to overcome low expectations and barriers to entry in the tech industry.

THE ESCAPE ARTIST recounts the journey of a woman who left home in search of a community - and found out how to build one - in the most unexpected places.
